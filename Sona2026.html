President Mutharika Outlines Bold Economic and Social Reforms in 2026 SONA

Lilongwe — In a packed session at the Parliament Building on Friday, President Arthur Peter Mutharika delivered the 2026 State of the Nation Address (SONA), setting out a comprehensive agenda aimed at steering Malawi out of persistent economic challenges and onto a path of sustainable growth.

The President acknowledged the severe economic crisis, including food insecurity, foreign exchange shortages, and high inflation — conditions that severely impacted households nationwide.

“We found a broken economy, and we acted swiftly,” Mutharika told Parliament, noting that inflation — once nearing 29% — is now projected to fall below 21% this year.

A major highlight was the focus on food security, with maize prices dropping after government interventions. The President reiterated: “No Malawian should die of hunger.”

Other plans included doubling national fuel storage, expanding electricity generation, strengthening industrial zones, overhauling mining policy, and investing in roads and markets. Public sector reforms and anti-corruption measures were also emphasized.

Reactions to the address have been mixed, but today’s SONA has set the tone for what the President described as a decisive era of recovery and national transformation.
